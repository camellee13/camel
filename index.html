The Effect of Doing and Messaging Pro-Environmental Behavior on Fostering the Behavior

Pro-environmental behavior has been widely promoted nowadays. While many empirical studies support that increasing social norms is an effective approach to motivate pro-environmental behavior, how it works in messaging is less understood. This study aimed to examine how one person sending his/her own pro- environmental behavior to a friend affects his/her friend's and his/her own future pro-environmental behavior. We conducted an in-situ study and found that receiving message of pro-environmental behavior didn’t promote the receiver’s behavior. Instead, the senders, who did the assigned behaviors and messaged the behavioral to one of their friends, increased the perceived ease and convenience and continued to took the action after intervention period. We came to the conclusion that doing and messaging pro- environmental behaviors has an effect on fostering the behavior.

Keywords: Pro-environmental behavior; messaging; theory of planned behavior; social norms

The perceptions of using instant interaction applications for enhancing peer discussion in a flipped classroom

This study attempted to use the application software installed on students' smartphones as an instant interactive tool in the classroom in a university course on basic computer science concepts. This study compared two different systems. One instant interaction application provided the students with multimedia feedback in time sequence, while the other showed the students' responses ranked by the number of students who 'liked' them. The students were divided into verbalisers and visualisers using a learning style questionnaire for comparison of students with different learning styles using the two systems. The results showed no significant difference between the two systems in terms of perceived usefulness, ease-of-use or enjoyment. The average frequency of the messages produced by the students using the multimedia-oriented system was higher than that for the like-ranking system on average at the beginning. However, the average frequency of messages produced by the students using the multimedia-oriented system decreased considerably week by week, and was fewer than those produced by the students using the like-ranking system in the fourth week. The results also showed that the visualisers perceived higher enjoyment than the verbalisers when using the interactive system showing responses in like-ranking sequence.

Keywords: interactive response system; learning style; perceived ease-of-use; perceived usefulness; perceived enjoyment.

發展與評估手機即時互動應用程式以提升課堂中同儕回饋

【摘要】 本研究直接使用大專生各自的手機作為課堂互動工具，比較三種不同導向即時互動手機應用程式，即人氣排行榜匿名導向(實驗組)、時序非匿名導向(對照組一)與時序匿名導向應用程式(對照組二)，代替需要額外購買的即時回饋系統 （Interactive Response System, IRS）在課堂上扮演即時同儕回饋的工具，本研究分析不同風格學生使用三種不同導向的應用程式在大一計算機概論課程的同儕互動，研究發現三種系統的都有高度有用性、易用性和愉悅性。研究發現視覺風格學生在三種回饋系統中有較高的認知愉悅性，最後，藉由行為分析發現學生確實能使用三種導向的互動系統於強化課堂即時討論。

【關鍵詞】 即時回饋、學習風格、易用性、有用性、愉悅性、行為分析

即時互動應用程式於課堂之互動行為分析

【摘要】本研究直接使用大專生各自的手機作為課堂互動工具，採用時序非匿名導向 即時互動手機應用程式，代替需要額外購買的即時回饋系統（Interactive Response System, IRS）在課堂上扮演即時同儕回饋的工具，本研究採用行為分析、互動次數比 較以了解學生在大一計算機概論課程的同儕互動，研究發現學生確實能使用該即時互 動手機應用程式於強化課堂即時討論。

【關鍵詞】 即時回饋、行為分析

應用手機即時互動程式於大學課堂中同儕回饋之接受度

This study attempted to use the application software installed in students’ smart phones to be the instantly interactive tools in the classroom. This study compared two different systems. One instant interaction application provided the students with multimedia feedbacks in time sequence, and the other one showed the responses of the students by the ranking of liking numbers which the students voted for the responses respectively. The results showed no significant difference between the two systems in terms of perceived usefulness, ease-of- use and enjoyment. This study also compared two different style students using different interactive systems in the basic concept of computer science class in the university. The average frequency of the messages in the multimedia-oriented system is higher than those in the other system on average at the beginning. However, the average frequency of messages decreased considerably week by week, and became lower than the other one in the fourth week. The results showed that the visualizers perceived higher enjoyment in comparison with the verbalizers in the interactive system showing responses in like-ranking sequence.

Keywords: Interactive Response System, learning style, perceived ease-of- use, perceived usefulness, perceived enjoyment
